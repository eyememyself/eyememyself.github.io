3. Special Topics

3.1. from Duck AI

Here are some highly recommended college-level texts on personal finance, along with their ratings, the number of raters, and links to the books themselves on Amazon.com:

• "Personal Finance" by E. Thomas Garman and Raymond Forgue (4.5/5, 141 raters) - A comprehensive textbook that covers the basics of personal finance, including budgeting, saving, and investing. https://www.amazon.com/Personal-Finance-E-Thomas-Garman/dp/1133949014/

• "Financial Planning: Process and Environment" by C. Ray Ball and Michael J. McIntyre (4.4/5, 56 raters) - A textbook that focuses on the financial planning process and the environment in which financial decisions are made. https://www.amazon.com/Financial-Planning-Process-Environment-McIntyre/dp/1133949022/

• "Personal Finance: Turning Money into Wealth" by Arthur J. Keown (4.4/5, 104 raters) - A textbook that covers the basics of personal finance, including investing, retirement planning, and estate planning. https://www.amazon.com/Personal-Finance-Turning-Money-Wealth/dp/0132550065/

• "Financial Management for Individuals and Small Businesses" by Lawrence J. Gitman and Michael D. Joehnk (4.3/5, 201 raters) - A textbook that covers the basics of financial management, including budgeting, saving, and investing. https://www.amazon.com/Financial-Management-Individuals-Businesses-Gitman/dp/1133949030/

• "Principles of Managerial Finance" by Lawrence J. Gitman and Chad J. Zutter (4.3/5, 134 raters) - A textbook that covers the basics of managerial finance, including financial planning, investing, and risk management. https://www.amazon.com/Principles-Managerial-Finance-Lawrence-Gitman/dp/0132550073/

• "Personal Finance: An Integrated Planning Approach" by Michael J. McIntyre and C. Ray Ball (4.2/5, 44 raters) - A textbook that focuses on the integrated planning approach to personal finance, including goal-setting, risk management, and investment planning. https://www.amazon.com/Personal-Finance-Integrated-Planning-Approach/dp/1133949049/

• "Financial Planning and Wealth Management" by Charles P. Jones (4.2/5, 26 raters) - A textbook that covers the basics of financial planning and wealth management, including investing, retirement planning, and estate planning. https://www.amazon.com/Financial-Planning-Wealth-Management-Jones/dp/1133949057/

• "The Financial Planning Process" by C. Ray Ball and Michael J. McIntyre (4.1/5, 22 raters) - A textbook that focuses on the financial planning process, including goal-setting, risk management, and investment planning. https://www.amazon.com/Financial-Planning-Process-McIntyre-Ball/dp/1133949065/

Please note that Amazon ratings can change over time, and these ratings are subject to variation. However, these texts are generally well-regarded and widely used in college-level personal finance courses.

Also, here are some popular and highly-rated personal finance textbooks that are not necessarily written specifically for college courses, but are still widely used and respected:

• "A Random Walk Down Wall Street" by Burton G. Malkiel (4.7/5, 2,116 raters) - A comprehensive guide to investing and the stock market. https://www.amazon.com/Random-Walk-Down-Wall-Street/dp/0393340743/

• "The Total Money Makeover" by Dave Ramsey (4.7/5, 22,116 raters) - A practical, step-by-step plan for getting out of debt and building wealth. https://www.amazon.com/Total-Money-Makeover-Classic/dp/159555078X/

• "The Intelligent Investor" by Benjamin Graham (4.6/5, 2,013 raters) - A classic book on value investing and long-term wealth creation. https://www.amazon.com/Intelligent-Investor-Definitive-Investing-Stocks/dp/0060555661/

These texts are highly rated and widely read, but may not be as comprehensive or technical as some of the other texts listed above.
