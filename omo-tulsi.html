  • General Information about Holy Basil Research:
  • Traditional Uses: Holy basil (also known as Tulsi) has a long history of use in Ayurvedic medicine for various health conditions, including stress, anxiety, diabetes, respiratory issues, and inflammation.
  • Phytochemicals: It is rich in various bioactive compounds, including essential oils (like eugenol, linalool, and caryophyllene), flavonoids (like orientin and vicenin), and phenolic acids (like rosmarinic acid).
  • Pharmacological Activities: Research has explored its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antidiabetic, antimicrobial, immunomodulatory, and stress-protective properties.
  • Growing Research Interest: The number of publications on holy basil has been steadily increasing, particularly in recent years, indicating a growing scientific interest in its potential health benefits. India and the United States have been major contributors to this research.
  • Focus Areas: Much of the research has focused on its effects on oxidative stress, diabetes, and its antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. More recent studies are also exploring its role in stress management and sleep.

However, the information above provides a good starting point for understanding significant research trends and relevant open-access publications on holy basil.
